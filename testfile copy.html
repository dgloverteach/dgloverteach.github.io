Instructions: Rounding to the Nearest Whole Number

1. Identify the digit in the ones place.

2. Look at the digit immediately to the right (the tenths place).

3. If the tenths digit is 5 or greater, round the ones digit up by 1.

4. If the tenths digit is less than 5, leave the ones digit unchanged.

5. All digits after the decimal point become zero or are removed.

Examples:

  • Example 1: Round 4.76 to the nearest whole number:
    Look at the tenths place (7). It’s greater than 5, so round up.
    The answer is 5.
  • Example 2: Round 3.14 to the nearest whole number:
    Look at the tenths place (1). It’s less than 5, so keep the ones digit unchanged.
    The answer is 3.
  • Example 3: Round 2.50 to the nearest whole number:
    Look at the tenths place (5). It’s 5, so round the ones digit up.
    The answer is 3.
  • Example 4: Round 8.23 to the nearest whole number:
    Look at the tenths place (2). It’s less than 5, so keep the ones digit unchanged.
    The answer is 8.

Instructions: Rounding to Decimal Places

1. Count the required number of decimal places from the left.

2. Look at the digit immediately to the right of the last required decimal place.

3. If this digit is 5 or greater, round the last required decimal place up by 1.

4. If this digit is less than 5, leave the decimal place unchanged.

5. Remove all digits beyond the required decimal places.

Examples:

  • Example 1: Round 3.14159 to 2 decimal places:
    Look at the third decimal place (1). It’s less than 5, so leave the second decimal place as it is.
    The answer is 3.14.
  • Example 2: Round 5.6789 to 3 decimal places:
    Look at the fourth decimal place (9). It’s greater than 5, so round the third decimal place up.
    The answer is 5.679.
  • Example 3: Round 9.9999 to 1 decimal place:
    Look at the second decimal place (9). It’s 9, so round the first decimal place up.
    The answer is 10.0.
  • Example 4: Round 0.45678 to 3 decimal places:
    Look at the fourth decimal place (7). It’s greater than 5, so round the third decimal place up.
    The answer is 0.457.

Instructions: Rounding to Significant Figures

1. Count the required number of significant figures starting from the leftmost non-zero digit.

2. Once you've identified the last significant figure, look at the next digit to the right.

3. If this digit is 5 or greater, round the last significant figure up by 1.

4. If it is less than 5, leave the last significant figure unchanged.

5. Replace any remaining digits with zeros or remove them (if after the decimal point).

Examples:

  • Example 1: Round 0.004567 to 2 significant figures:
    The first two significant figures are 4 and 5. Look at the next digit (6). Round the 5 up to 6.
    The answer is 0.0046.
  • Example 2: Round 12345 to 3 significant figures:
    The first three significant figures are 1, 2, and 3. Look at the fourth digit (4). It’s less than 5, so keep 3 unchanged.
    The answer is 12300.
  • Example 3: Round 789.654 to 4 significant figures:
    The first four significant figures are 7, 8, 9, and 6. Look at the fifth digit (5). Round the 6 up.
    The answer is 789.7.
  • Example 4: Round 0.00034567 to 3 significant figures:
    The first three significant figures are 3, 4, and 5. Look at the fourth digit (6). Round the 5 up.
    The answer is 0.000346.
