Instructions for Circuit Calculations

Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law states that the current (I) flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the voltage (V) across it and inversely proportional to the resistance (R). Use the formula:

I = V / R

Voltage Drop Calculation

The voltage drop across a resistor can be calculated using:

V = I × R

Examples:

  • Ohm's Law Example: If V = 12V and R = 3Ω, then I = 12V / 3Ω = 4A
  • Voltage Drop Example: If I = 2A and R = 5Ω, then V = 2A × 5Ω = 10V
