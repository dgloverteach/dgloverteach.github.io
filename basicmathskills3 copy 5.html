Instructions - How to Add Numbers

Addition is the process of combining two or more numbers to find their total. Here’s how you can perform addition:

Steps for Addition

1. Align the Numbers: Write the numbers you want to add one below the other, aligning them by their rightmost digits.

2. Start from the Right: Begin adding from the rightmost digit (the ones place). If the sum is greater than 9, carry over the extra value to the next column on the left.

3. Move Left: Continue adding each column, moving left, and including any carry-over from the previous column.

4. Write the Total: Once all columns are added, write down the total sum at the bottom.

Example 1:

Step Action
1 Align the numbers:
   123
+ 456
2 Add the ones place: 3 + 6 = 9 (write 9)
3 Add the tens place: 2 + 5 = 7 (write 7)
4 Add the hundreds place: 1 + 4 = 5 (write 5)
5 Result: 579

Example 2 (With Carry Over):

Step Action
1 Align the numbers:
   376
+ 487
2 Add the ones place: 6 + 7 = 13 (write 3 and carry over 1)
3 Add the tens place: 7 + 8 = 15 (plus 1 from carry-over = 16; write 6 and carry over 1)
4 Add the hundreds place: 3 + 4 = 7 (plus 1 from carry-over = 8; write 8)
5 Result: 863

Summary

  • Align the Numbers: Place them one below the other.
  • Add from Right to Left: Start with the ones place.
  • Carry Over if Necessary: Move any extra value to the next column.
  • Write the Total: The sum is your final answer.

Instructions - How to Subtract Numbers

Subtraction is the process of finding the difference between two numbers. Here’s how you can perform subtraction:

Steps for Subtraction

1. Align the Numbers: Write the numbers you want to subtract one below the other, aligning them by their rightmost digits.

2. Start from the Right: Begin subtracting from the rightmost digit (the ones place). If the top number is smaller, you will need to borrow from the next column on the left.

3. Move Left: Continue subtracting each column, moving left, and including any borrowed value.

4. Write the Difference: Once all columns are subtracted, write down the difference at the bottom.

Example 1:

Step Action
1 Align the numbers:
    789
−  456
2 Subtract the ones place: 9 − 6 = 3 (write 3)
3 Subtract the tens place: 8 − 5 = 3 (write 3)
4 Subtract the hundreds place: 7 − 4 = 3 (write 3)
5 Result: 333

Example 2 (With Borrowing):

Step Action
1 Align the numbers:
    705
−  468
2 Subtract the ones place: 5 − 8 = -3 (borrow 1 from tens place, so 15 − 8 = 7, write 7)
3 Subtract the tens place: 0 − 6 (after borrowing, it’s 9 − 6 = 3, write 3)
4 Subtract the hundreds place: 6 − 4 = 2 (write 2)
5 Result: 237

Summary

  • Align the Numbers: Place them one below the other.
  • Subtract from Right to Left: Start with the ones place.
  • Borrow if Necessary: Borrow from the next column when the top digit is smaller.
  • Write the Difference: The result is your final answer.

Instructions - How to Multiply Numbers

Multiplication is the process of adding a number to itself a certain number of times. Here’s how you can perform multiplication:

Steps for Multiplication

1. Write the Numbers: Write the numbers you want to multiply one below the other, with the larger number on top.

2. Multiply the Digits: Start multiplying from the rightmost digit of the bottom number, moving to the left.

3. Carry Over if Needed: If the multiplication of two digits results in a number greater than 9, write down the unit digit and carry over the ten digit to the next column on the left.

4. Add the Products: Add all the partial products, taking into account the place value (ones, tens, hundreds, etc.).

Example 1: Single-Digit Multiplication

Step Action
1 Align the numbers:
    7
×    3
2 Multiply 7 by 3: 21

Example 2: Double-Digit Multiplication

Step Action
1 Align the numbers:
    23
×    15
2 Multiply 3 by 15:
3 × 15 = 45
3 Multiply 2 (tens place) by 15:
20 × 15 = 300
4 Add the results: 45 + 300 = 345

Example 3: Triple-Digit Multiplication

Step Action
1 Align the numbers:
    123
×     456
2 Multiply 3 by 456:
3 × 456 = 1368
3 Multiply 2 (tens place) by 456:
20 × 456 = 9120
4 Multiply 1 (hundreds place) by 456:
100 × 456 = 45600
5 Add the results: 1368 + 9120 + 45600 = 56088

Example 4: Decimal Multiplication

Step Action
1 Align the numbers:
    12.3
×     4.56
2 Ignore the decimal points and multiply 123 by 456:
123 × 456 = 56088
3 Count the total number of decimal places in the original numbers (3):
Place the decimal point three places from the right: 56.088
4 Final result: 12.3 × 4.56 = 56.088

Summary

  • Write the Numbers: Place them one below the other.
  • Multiply from Right to Left: Start with the ones place of the bottom number.
  • Carry Over if Necessary: Carry over when the product exceeds 9.
  • Add the Products: Sum up all the partial products.
  • For Decimals: Multiply ignoring the decimals, then adjust the final result by placing the decimal point.

Instructions - How to Perform Division

Division is the process of finding out how many times one number is contained within another. Here’s how you can perform division:

Steps for Long Division

1. Divide: Determine how many times the divisor can fit into the dividend. Write the quotient above the line.

2. Multiply: Multiply the divisor by the quotient and write the result below the dividend.

3. Subtract: Subtract the result from the dividend to find the remainder.

4. Bring Down: Bring down the next digit of the dividend (if any) and repeat the process.

5. Continue: Repeat the steps until all digits have been brought down and the division is complete.

Example 1:

Step Action
1 Divide: 487 ÷ 4. The divisor (4) goes into 48 (the first two digits of the dividend) 12 times. Write 12 above the line.
2 Multiply: 4 × 12 = 48. Write 48 below 48.
3 Subtract: 48 - 48 = 0. Write 0 below the line.
4 Bring Down: Bring down the next digit (7) to make 07.
5 Divide: 07 ÷ 4 = 1 (quotient). Write 1 above the line.
6 Multiply: 4 × 1 = 4. Write 4 below 7.
7 Subtract: 7 - 4 = 3. Write 3 below the line.
8 Result: 121 with a remainder of 3.

Example 2:

Step Action
1 Divide: 1356 ÷ 6. The divisor (6) goes into 13 (the first two digits of the dividend) 2 times. Write 2 above the line.
2 Multiply: 6 × 2 = 12. Write 12 below 13.
3 Subtract: 13 - 12 = 1. Write 1 below the line.
4 Bring Down: Bring down the next digit (5) to make 15.
5 Divide: 15 ÷ 6 = 2. Write 2 above the line.
6 Multiply: 6 × 2 = 12. Write 12 below 15.
7 Subtract: 15 - 12 = 3. Write 3 below the line.
8 Bring Down: Bring down the next digit (6) to make 36.
9 Divide: 36 ÷ 6 = 6. Write 6 above the line.
10 Multiply: 6 × 6 = 36. Write 36 below 36.
11 Subtract: 36 - 36 = 0. Write 0 below the line.
12 Result: 226 with no remainder.

Summary

  • Divide: Find out how many times the divisor fits into the dividend.
  • Multiply: Multiply the divisor by the quotient.
  • Subtract: Subtract the result from the dividend.
  • Bring Down: Bring down the next digit (if any).
  • Repeat: Continue until all digits are processed.
